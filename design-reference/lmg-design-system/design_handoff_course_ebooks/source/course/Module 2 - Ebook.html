Go on offenseChapter 2.0

Stop waiting to be discovered.

In Module 1 you got your numbers. Now we put them to work — and the first job is the one most creators skip: building a system that brings the right brands to you, instead of waiting and hoping.

Here's the trap almost everyone falls into, no matter their size. They're reactive. They wait for a DM. They take whatever inbound lands. They have no real say in which brands they work with — they just react to whoever shows up. A 300K-follower creator can be every bit as reactive as a 15K one; the only difference is the size of the inbox.

Being reactive means your income depends on luck and other people's timing — and that you're often working with brands you'd never have chosen. Going proactive flips that. Instead of waiting, you generate a steady flow of opportunities from the brands you actually want.

And the way you do that is by running three channels at once. There are exactly three ways a brand opportunity reaches you. Most creators lean on one and ignore the rest. The ones who build reliable income run all three in parallel — so when one is quiet, the others keep producing.

Channel one · Direct outreachChapter 2.1

Your target-brand list.

Direct outreach puts you in the driver's seat — it's how you land collaborations with specific brands you're genuinely excited about, rather than whoever happens to find you. Build the list in four moves.

1
Aim for 20–30

Build the list

Three criteria for getting on it: brands you already use and love, brands whose aesthetic matches your content, and brands whose customers look like your audience — this is where your Module 1 audience snapshot earns its keep.

2
Where to find them

Look wide, not just big

Look at what creators similar to you already partner with — those brands clearly invest in creator marketing. And don't limit yourself to the giants: smaller or newer brands without a mature influencer program are often easier to start with and grow into something long-term.

3
Before you reach out

Research each one

Their recent campaigns, their latest launches, and where partnership decisions seem to be made. You're hunting for specifics you can reference — specifics are what separate a real pitch from a generic one.

4
The move most creators miss

Warm them up first

Before you pitch, engage genuinely on social — thoughtful comments, sharing their content when it resonates, tagging them when you really use the product. By the time your outreach lands, you're not a cold stranger; you're a name they've already seen.

The asset that sells for youChapter 2.2

Your media kit.

Before you go to market, you need the one document that does your selling for you: a single, visually clean page that lets a brand understand your value in thirty seconds. Brands forward media kits internally, so it has to stand on its own. This is where your Module 1 numbers go to work.

1Who you are

Short bio

Your niche and what makes you valuable, in one or two sentences.

2From Module 1

Audience demographics

Real numbers and percentages — age, location, gender split, interests.

3From Module 1

Engagement metrics

Your pricing-power number — the rate that proves an audience that responds.

4Show, don't tell

Best content + case studies

A few of your strongest pieces, plus brief results if you have them.

→ Lead with engagement and audience fit, not raw follower count — that framing is your edge at any size.
The opening line that wins

Not "50,000 followers." Instead: "50,000 followers, 6.8% engagement, audience 82% women 25–40 actively shopping for sustainable products." The second tells a brand your exact customer is here, and they're paying attention. Update your kit every quarter so it never quietly undersells you.

Channel two · Your matchmakersChapter 2.3

Platforms & networks.

The second channel does some of the work for you — quietly connecting you to brands in the background while you run your outreach. Sort out which is which, and put your energy in the right places.

1
Set up a strong profile

Influencer platforms

They match creators and brands and handle contracts, approvals, and payments. You apply to campaigns or get discovered by brands searching for your criteria. Some specialize by niche — research which are most active in yours right now.

2
Run these in parallel

Affiliate networks

Access to thousands of commission programs. The per-sale payout is smaller than a flat fee, but affiliate income is passive and compounds — especially on evergreen content. A smart background layer under everything else.

3
A later-stage option

Talent agencies

They take ~15–20% to connect you with premium deals, negotiate, and handle contracts — but they're selective and sign proven creators. Don't chase representation early; build your foundation first and the good ones come to you.

→ Platforms and programs change constantly. Treat any list of names as a starting map, not gospel — go where your niche actually lives.

Channel three · Be findableChapter 2.4

Inbound discovery.

The third channel makes it effortless for brands to find and book you when they're already searching. The best part: most of it is a one-time setup that works for you forever.

1Do these today

Optimize your profile

A professional business email in your bio, a Business/Professional account, and an explicit "Partnership inquiries:" line. Remove all doubt that you're open.

2Get surfaced

Consistent hashtags

Industry and niche tags help brands find you — but serve your audience first. And never put #ad on content that isn't sponsored.

3Your home base

A simple landing page

One page: media kit, portfolio, short bio, contact form. Squarespace, Wix, or Carrd. Then link it everywhere — it signals you run a business.

4Most underrated

Your creator network

Brands ask creators they trust for referrals. Be a generous part of a community and you'll be the name that comes up. Abundance, not competition.

→ You now have a pipeline engine, not a waiting game. But more opportunities mean more decisions — and not every brand deserves a yes. Module 3 builds your filter.
