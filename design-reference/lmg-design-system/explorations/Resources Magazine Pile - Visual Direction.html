Reading against the references

This matches the photos on the three things that matter: square corners so the covers read as printed magazines, a dense clustered pile (heavy overlap, casual angles, page-edge thickness and layered shadows for real depth) rather than a spread-out scatter, and a fully contained composition — every magazine sits wholly inside the section with clear margins around the pile, so nothing is sliced at an edge. Depth and abundance read from covers overlapping each other and the page-block edges peeking out beneath the top few.

It stays on-brief otherwise: no featured hero (the top covers are simply the top of the pile, not promoted), text only at the header and a one-line caption, and one destination — /resources. Dials if needed: add or remove a peeking cover, tighten the cluster further, or warm the surface tint. The ambitious next step is the same as before — drag-to-toss physics so a visitor can physically shove the pile around (higher build cost; the static pile here already sells the feel).
