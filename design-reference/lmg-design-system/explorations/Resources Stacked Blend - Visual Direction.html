Featured + Riffle, stacked

Re-architected from side-by-side to vertical stack so neither half is squeezed: the featured hero gets the full section width for a roomy, un-cramped text block, and the riffling library spreads across the full width too — more covers visible, more depth, more room for the motion to breathe. Two stacking orders compared, both light and dark. Motion at full width is the bold auto-riffle, now fanned across the whole band. Reduced-motion falls back to a calm static gallery.

Which order reads better?

Recommend Order A — Featured on top

Order A wins for a homepage section. The eye enters at the top, so the flagship publication, its value, and the primary Download — Free PDF all land first — the section states what it is before it dazzles. The riffling library then reads as a natural "and there are 22 more" invitation, and its motion lives in the lower half where it pulls the scroll downward toward the next section rather than competing with the headline.

Order B is the stronger choice only when breadth is the pitch — e.g. a dedicated /resources landing page or a returning-visitor surface, where opening on a wide wall of beautiful covers signals "deep library" instantly and the featured hero becomes a confident closing anchor. On the homepage, though, leading with motion risks the visitor riffling covers before they've registered the flagship or its free download.

Either way, stacking fixes the original problem: the featured text block is no longer cramped, and the library — now full width — fans more covers across the band with the motion given room to breathe. Covers stay the hero, the audience accent line holds the color code (blue brands · pink creators · yellow universal), and View all 23 resources → sits in the header on both.
