Visual Direction · Homepage §3

Latest Insights

An editorial "latest stories" strip that surfaces the three newest articles and routes into a 54-piece library. It belongs to the same launchpad family as the reach map and the industry browse above it — photography-led, accent-coded — but reads as a magazine's front-of-book, not a blog roll. The cover photo is the hero of every card; the category chip and read time are the only chrome. Explored in light and dark, with the card anatomy resolved first, then two layouts: an even three-up and a lead-plus-two.

THE CARD

Chip & read time, three ways

Where the category and read time live — and how a card holds up when the excerpt is missing.

A · Chip on cover Recommended

Category rides the photo bottom-left, so the cover stays the hero and the body is pure editorial: title, excerpt, then a read-time + read-more footer. The footer pins to the bottom, so a missing excerpt never breaks the rhythm.

B · Meta row above title

Chip and read time share one row beneath the cover, above the title. Tidy and conventional, but it stacks two type rows before you reach the headline and leaves the photo plain.

C · Split footer

Chip on the cover, read time alone in a ruled footer. Clean, but the divider rule adds a line of chrome the editorial direction is trying to shed.

Even three-up

Recommendation: ship card treatment A (chip on cover) in the even three-up layout. It keeps the photography full-bleed and the body purely editorial, matches the rhythm of the industry-browse grid directly above without copying it, and treats all three latest posts as equally current — the honest read for a strip that updates weekly. Lead-plus-two is the alternate for moments when we want to feature an editor's pick. Category chips use the brand trio as accents — blue Strategy, pink Platforms, yellow Industry — extendable to the full category set. The "View all 54 articles →" control carries the depth signal; the count updates live. One component, one light/dark token swap, no author byline or view counts.
