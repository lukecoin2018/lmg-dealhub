Introduction

Why market-specific strategy matters.

A creator who performs exceptionally well in London may be completely unknown in Dubai. A content format that goes viral on TikTok in New York may fall flat in Paris. A campaign that complies with ASA regulations in the UK may violate FTC requirements if the same content is repurposed for the US market.

The differences between influencer marketing markets are not cosmetic — they are structural, and they require genuine strategic adaptation rather than surface-level localisation.

This guide provides market-by-market intelligence for six of the world's most important influencer marketing cities. Use it to understand what makes each market distinctive, what the most effective strategies look like for that market, and what mistakes to avoid.

Each market profile covers the same dimensions — platform landscape, cultural calendar, key niches, regulatory considerations, and what brands consistently get right and wrong — so you can read any city in isolation or scan across all six.

01Market Overview

Dubai is one of the most distinctive influencer marketing markets in the world. The UAE's social media penetration — among the highest globally — combined with a young, affluent, internationally connected population, creates a market where influencer marketing is central to how brands reach consumers.

The creator community spans Arabic, English, Hindi, and more — an English-language creator serving Western expatriates is a fundamentally different proposition from an Arabic-language creator serving Gulf-native audiences. Treating them as interchangeable is the most common mistake brands make here.

01Market Overview

London is one of the world's most sophisticated and culturally influential influencer markets. The city's creators produce content that punches above the UK's population weight — fashion, beauty, food, and lifestyle audiences extend across Europe and North America.

Cross-Market Strategy

Building a multi-city programme.

10 / 12

For brands building international influencer marketing programmes, market sequencing matters. A recommended approach when starting from scratch:

01

Start with your primary consumer market.

Build a functioning programme in your most important revenue market before expanding. Get the strategy, creator relationships, and operational infrastructure right in one market before replicating it.

02

Expand to adjacent markets with shared infrastructure.

London and New York share language and broadly similar regulatory frameworks — brands active in one can expand to the other with less adaptation than entering Dubai or Paris simultaneously.

03

Enter culturally distinct markets with local expertise.

Dubai, Paris, and Miami (for Spanish-language reach) require genuine local market knowledge. Partner with an agency that has active creator relationships and regulatory expertise rather than attempting to operate from a distance.

Coordinating multi-city campaigns — a checklist

  • Lead-market strategy. Define which city drives the campaign direction, with other markets adapting rather than running independent campaigns.
  • Creator network consistency. Where possible, work with creators across markets who share aesthetic sensibility and audience demographic profile.
  • Compliance management. Each market has distinct regulatory requirements — build a compliance checklist per market rather than applying a single global standard.
  • Content localisation. Adapt content for cultural context, language, and platform behaviour in each market — not just translation but genuine creative localisation.
