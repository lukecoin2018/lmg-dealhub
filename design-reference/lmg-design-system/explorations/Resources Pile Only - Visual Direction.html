Reading against the goal

Dropping the hero removes the last "big thing + small things" tension — it's now one uniform, abundant pile, which is more on-concept and lets the artwork do all the talking. The silhouette fix is what makes it land: the same covers, re-cut to a tall 3:4 with crisp corners, instantly read as printed magazines and reports rather than app icons, so a strewn stack of them feels like a real table you'd rummage.

Text lives only at the edges — header up top, a one-line caption at the bottom — so nothing floats in the middle competing with the covers. The pile bleeds off all four edges to imply it continues, and every cover is one tap from the full library at /resources. Still pushed intentionally messy per the brief; angle range (±20°), overlap density, and back-of-pile dim are the dials to tame later. The bold optional next step remains drag-to-toss physics so visitors can physically shove covers around.
