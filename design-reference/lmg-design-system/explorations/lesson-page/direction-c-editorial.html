The Partnership Landscape
ii. The money

The six ways creators get paid

Most creators know about sponsored posts, and maybe affiliate links. But there are six distinct revenue streams, and even established creators routinely leave several of them on the table — not because they're hard, but because no one ever laid them out in one place.

  1. Sponsored contentA flat fee to create a post, video, or story featuring a brand. The familiar one.
  2. Affiliate partnershipsA commission on the sales you drive — usually 5–30% — that earns on autopilot.
  3. Brand ambassadorshipsA longer 6–12 month relationship where you become a recurring face. Usually the goal.
  4. Product giftingFree product in exchange for content. Good early for portfolio and relationships.
  5. Content licensingGetting paid again for work you already made — the most commonly missed stream.
  6. Speaking & UGCYour expertise and your craft, sold directly — beyond your own feed.
iii. The levers

What actually drives your rates

Here is the question that unlocks everything: if two creators have the same follower count, why does one command triple the rate? Because rates aren't set by size. They're set by five factors — and once you can see them, you stop competing on follower count and start competing on value.

  1. EngagementBrands pay for an audience that interacts and buys, not one that scrolls past. The biggest lever.
  2. Content qualityYour eye, your storytelling, your craft. Your calling card.
  3. ProfessionalismReply on time, hit deadlines, be easy to work with. The most underrated lever — and the easiest to win on.
  4. Track recordResults you've driven and words from past partners. It compounds with every deal.
  5. Audience understandingNot just how many follow you, but who they are and what they want. It ties everything together.

Professionalism is the easiest lever to win on — and the one creators most often ignore.

iv. Your baseline

Your pricing-power number

You don't need a spreadsheet to find out where you stand. The fastest signal is your engagement rate, and it takes about five minutes. Take a typical recent post, add the likes and comments together, divide by your follower count, and multiply by one hundred.

The engagement-rate formula
( Likes + CommentsFollowers )×100=Your rate

A creator at 6% engagement can defend a meaningfully higher rate than a same-sized creator at 1% — because the brand is paying for an audience that actually responds. Whatever your number is today, it's your honest baseline, and it's the first thing a brand should feel when they meet your work.
