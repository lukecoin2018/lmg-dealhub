Visual Direction · Homepage §4 · Messy-pile revision

One messy table

The stacked carousel got too tidy — and read as two segments. This revision brings back Direction 3's physical "magazines spread on a table" feeling and pushes it: the other 22 covers are strewn in a real pile — wildly varied angles, irregular overlap, depth from soft shadows and dimming, some cut off at the edges so the pile clearly continues. The flagship rests on top of that same pile, no divider, no seam. The concept: "here's a great one to read first — and look, there's a whole pile of others." The pile is a teaser and a doorway, not a selector — hover lifts a cover; clicking any cover opens /resources. Nothing swaps the hero.

Reading against the goal

Does the featured read as "start here" while the pile pulls you to explore? Yes — the hero is the only crisp, upright-ish, fully-captioned object with the bright yellow Download, so the eye resolves it first; everything else is deliberately tilted, overlapped, and dimmed toward the back, which reads as "more, lots more" rather than competing choices. Because the pile literally underlaps the hero's cover and there's no rule between them, it scans as one messy table, not two stacked blocks.

Guardrails kept: the hero text rides a soft halo so it stays legible no matter what cover edges sit behind it; the pile is a doorway (one destination, /resources) not an on-page selector, so it never fights the hero for "which do I pick." This is intentionally pushed too messy per the brief — easy dials to tame later: reduce angle range (±20°→±12°), thin the overlap, or lighten the back-of-pile dim. The bold next step, if wanted, is drag-to-toss physics so a visitor can actually shove covers around — higher build cost (pointer velocity + collision), noted but not required.
