B

The Opportunity Scorecard

Your reusable tool

Score each criterion 1–5. The first two are deal-breakers — score them 1–2 and it's a no, regardless of the total.

Deal being evaluated
CriterionScore (1–5)
1Values & content-style alignment (would my audience be surprised?)⚑ Deal-breaker
2I can authentically recommend it (I use/love it, or will test it)⚑ Deal-breaker
3My audience genuinely benefits / is interested
4Fits my long-term brand direction
5I'm comfortable being associated with the company
6Audience fit (demographic + psychographic match)
Total0/ 30
24–30Strong yes — pursue & price it well (M4).
18–23Proceed — fix the gaps first.
< 18Likely a no — the fit isn't there.
⚑ 1–2Decline — any deal-breaker, whatever the total.
B3If you scored 18–23, what specifically needs to be addressed to turn this into a yes?
Example · the tempting trap

A detox-tea brand offers double your rate. Values fit = 2, authentic recommendation = 1. Two deal-breakers in the danger zone → decline, no matter the total. Reuse a fresh scorecard for every opportunity — it's a tool for the life of your business.
